## Design Context

### Users
B2B buyers for cafés de especialidad, roasteries, and food-service distributors in Spanish-speaking markets, evaluating Japanese matcha for wholesale contracts. They arrive with operational intent: assess region, variety, harvest, MOQ, and price per kg. They are not retail consumers. Decisions are made by purchasing/ops staff who need credibility signals, documented traceability, and a clear path to quote/contract. Typical sessions are short and goal-oriented — browse catalog → inspect blend → request quote via form or WhatsApp.

### Brand Personality
**Refined · precise · warm.** Editorial restraint with an artisanal undertone. Confident, technical, and quiet — never loud, never decorative, never chatty. Voice is short-declarative Spanish with mono-typeset metadata (lot numbers, MOQ, region). The interface should feel like a wholesale lookbook, not a DTC storefront.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Editorial minimalism with Japanese rhythm** — vertical rules, mono labels, generous negative space, asymmetric grids, numbered sections (Nº 01, 02…).
- **Mixed theme**: dark hero and key CTAs (near-black, warm off-white ink), cream content body (tinted warm neutral, no pure #fff).
- **Strip CJK characters** (工舎, 日本, 日本語) across all pages — keep the stylistic structure those tokens provided (meta lines, mono eyebrows, hairline rules) but replace CJK content with romaji or Spanish equivalents (e.g., "Uji", "Shizuoka", "Edición 001"). Keep the word KŌSHA as wordmark.
- **Typography**: DM Sans (sans, variable) + DM Mono for metadata/eyebrows. Display weight 700 or tight tracking for large headings.
- **Color**: warm neutrals only — ink near-black (#0d0d0b or oklch equivalent), off-white cream (#fdfaf6 range), matcha-green accent used sparingly as a dot/rule/highlight, no gradients, no neon, no cyan-on-dark.
- **Imagery**: product photography on dark matte surfaces; catalog card-selector uses black background with tea-tin-style imagery.

### Anti-References (do NOT look like)
- Generic SaaS/AI template (gradient heroes, purple-to-blue accents, rounded feature cards, hero-metric layout).
- Wellness/lifestyle/Instagram brand (pastel, hand-lettered, influencer-leaning).
- Touristy Japan kitsch (cherry blossoms, torii gates, decorative kanji as pattern).
- Luxury perfume / fashion editorial (cold, aspirational, overly sparse with giant serifs).

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint signals credibility.** Every element must earn its place. When in doubt, remove. Negative space > ornament.
2. **Metadata as design.** Mono labels, numbered sections, hairline rules, lot-style typography do the work decoration would otherwise do.
3. **One section = one viewport.** All pages size sections to fit viewport height (100svh, with safe content scaling), so the site reads like a deck of cards rather than a long scroll.
4. **Warm neutrals, never pure.** Tint all "black" and "white" toward the matcha/cream palette. Pure #000 and #fff are banned.
5. **Japanese rhythm, not Japanese decoration.** Keep editorial structure (asymmetry, mono eyebrows, vertical rules) but never use CJK characters, cherry-blossom motifs, or travel-poster Japonism.
6. **Commercial intent is first-class.** Every page offers a clear path to quote (WhatsApp, form, catalog CTA). The WhatsApp button is persistent.
